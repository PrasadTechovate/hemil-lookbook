KNOW ME, A LITTLE

How I see · How I decide · How I collaborate

I look for where the emotional weight is sitting — who holds power and who is exposed. That tells me where the camera should stand.
The camera moves only when the character’s perspective shifts. If nothing changes internally, the camera stays quiet.
I reduce the scene to one believable source and shape around it. Less fixtures, more control.
I first understand what the scene must do emotionally. Once that is clear, I design the visual language to support it. References come later—only to align teams, not to decide taste.
I assume nothing will be fixed later. Clean plates, consistent exposure, and clear separation of light layers. VFX should enhance the image, not repair it.
Visual continuity, exposure safety, or whether something will cut together. That mental space should be reserved for actors and story.
